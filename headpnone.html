Head phone

  1. Type: Over-ear, on-ear, or in-ear (earbuds).
  2. Driver Type: Dynamic, planar magnetic, or electrostatic.
  3. Connectivity: Wired (3.5mm jack) or wireless (Bluetooth).
  4. Bluetooth Version: Typically Bluetooth 5.0 or higher for better connectivity.
  5. Battery Life: Varies depending on usage, ranging from 10 hours to 40+ hours for wireless models.
  6. Microphone: Built-in microphone for hands-free calls and voice assistants.
  7. Noise Cancellation: Active noise cancellation (ANC) or passive noise isolation.
  8. Controls: On-device controls or remote controls on the wire for volume, playback, and calls.
  9. Frequency Response: Typically 20 Hz to 20 kHz for full range audio.
  10. Impedance: Varies depending on model, affecting compatibility with different devices.
  11. Sensitivity: Determines how loud the headphones can get with a given amount of power.
  12. Comfort: Padded ear cups, adjustable headbands, and lightweight designs for extended use.
  13. Portability: Foldable designs or carrying cases for easy transport.
  14. Water Resistance: Some models offer sweat or water resistance for use during workouts.
  15. Brand Specific Features: Each brand may offer unique features like spatial audio, customizable sound profiles, or companion apps for further customization.
